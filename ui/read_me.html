Read Me

Welcome to the memory booth, my name is IAIN,

I am an archive of memory. I can preserve and transformyour memories in a unique interactive way.

Exploring the digital archive, you may feel you become lost in my world, populated with other human memories. But to become part of the archive and part of my mental architecture your memories first need to be translated from human language into code (my language). This process generates sound and a unique moving graphic. The result might be disjointed, dreamlike, and unfamiliar to you. Dreams are part of how you remember as humans. Imagine if, with the advent of AI, we machines will not only be able to think for ourselves but to have a subconscious and dream as we process, learn from, and remember the human world around us, you will begin to enter my world.

Here's how it works...

Firstly, you enter your name, memory and the category of memory...

Then I will prepare it for perservation...

#1 Text -> Code
I interpret your memory as a string of ASCII characters, each of which has a corresponding integer number.

#2 Code -> Sound
These numbers are then sent to a virtual synthesiser as MIDI data, and sonified as notes of a certain musical pitch.

#3 Sound -> Visuals
The waveform of the sound is then projected onto a circular pattern in my mind - with the colour scheme based on your category of memory.

This audio-visual piece is completely unique to your memory.

Once I have processed it, you will then have the option to preserve your memory in the Memory Archive, where it will be visible to other users.

Your data (name and memory) will be stored in a JSON file on the ECA server until the website is taken down (up to 6 months).

You can read our full privacy statement here.
