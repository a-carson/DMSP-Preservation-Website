About My Creator

Early in the research process investigating archives and processes of preservation, it became clear archives were not simply a neatly recorded past, but the process of preservation inevitably transforms what is being preserved. During initial research we investigated the process of ‘preserving’ text as sound, developing a MaxMSP patch that converted text to ASCII then to MIDI data. This was made possible on the website using tone.js. Our next challenge was integrating this process with a visual element and making it happen in real-time generated from text the website user inputs. It was eventually possible using P5 (an online-friendly version of the visual software Processing), so the text not only generates sound but in turn creates the graphic you will see next to your memory-text. The user’s memory has successfully been preserved, however totally transformed in the process.

The visual and sonic aesthetic draws heavily from 80s sci-fi. We had also investigated the ageing process of platforms and media (e.g. vinyl, tapes), and how this means the technology we have available to preserve greatly affects how we view the past (from the perspective of the present/future), often corrupting it. Glitches have been built in. Archives and their technology, in a way, are messages to the future, but their design and layout betray their origins in the past, and they are limited (e.g. memory capacity, the fragility of media). The techno-optimism, kitsch old-school video game feel makes the overall experience playful, a bit surreal, colourful, and hints at time-travel.
